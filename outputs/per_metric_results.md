## 1. Revenue per user (USD/day)

Family: heavy_tailed_continuous. Expected winner (design intent): `winsor_99_upper`. Selected winner (from the run): **`winsor_99_upper`**.

Outlier story: The tail holds BOTH genuine signal (real high spenders, whom the treatment actually moves by converting more users into big spenders) AND contamination (physically impossible values from a logging bug, far above any real user). Because the effect lives in the tail, clipping too hard shaves genuine spenders and biases the effect. Light one-sided winsorization at the 99th percentile removes only the extreme contamination while keeping the genuine spenders, so it best preserves how the early reading tracks the eventual estimate (the highest predictiveness) without over-clipping, and it is the method selected here. It is also the least-biased handling with correct coverage of the true effect, which the ground-truth validation confirms after the fact. Aggressive clipping (for example the 80th or 90th percentile, or a 3 SD cap) has lower variance but collapses the between-experiment signal, so its predictiveness R squared falls even though its readings look stable, and it is not selected. Business-rule removal of the impossible values (drop_impossible) is a clean, low-bias alternative.

## 2. Engagement events per user (count/day)

Family: heavy_tailed_count. Expected winner (design intent): `winsor_95_2s`. Selected winner (from the run): **`trim_95_upper`**.

Outlier story: The tail is contamination (bots and scrapers) that is NOT caused by the treatment and carries no product signal. The real treatment effect is a modest multiplicative lift spread across the human population, including legitimate power users. There is no natural business ceiling for a count, so a fixed business rule either clips real power users or misses low-end bots. An adaptive distribution-based method that clips or removes the extreme tail (an aggressive winsorization or an equivalent upper trim) removes the bot influence while keeping the human signal. Expected winner: aggressive winsorization or upper trimming.
